    <p>A good budget PC build is not about buying the cheapest parts. It is about spending where performance matters and cutting costs where the difference is small.</p>
<p>The best strategy is usually to prioritize the GPU, keep the power supply reputable, and avoid overspending on a flashy case or unnecessary motherboard features.</p>
<h2>Step 1: Set the target</h2>
<p>Pick a resolution and refresh rate first. A 1080p, 60-144 Hz target is usually the sweet spot for value.</p>
<h2>Step 2: Balance CPU and GPU</h2>
<p>For budget builds, the GPU usually drives the experience. A strong midrange card or a good used GPU often beats spending extra on the CPU.</p>
<h2>Step 3: Don’t cheap out on the power supply</h2>
<p>A bad PSU can ruin a cheap build. Pick a reputable 80+ unit from a known line and size it correctly.</p>
<h2>Step 4: Prefer used parts where they make sense</h2>
<p>Used GPUs, cases, and storage can save a lot. Skip used parts for things that are risky or heavily worn unless you know what you are buying.</p>
<h2>Example parts direction</h2>
<ul><li>6-core CPU or better</li><li>32 GB RAM if prices are close, 16 GB minimum</li><li>1 TB SSD</li><li>Midrange used GPU for 1080p</li><li>Quality 650W PSU</li></ul>
<p>Keep the final build quiet, stable, and easy to upgrade.</p>
